Ask someone what makes a lawn look *professionally maintained* and most people can't quite put a finger on it. The grass is green, sure — but so is the neighbor's. Nine times out of ten, the real difference is the **edge**.

## Edging is the frame

Think of your lawn as a picture and the edge as the frame. A crisp, defined line where the grass meets the driveway, the sidewalk, and the beds reads as *intentional*. It tells the eye that someone cared. Even a modest lawn looks sharp with clean edges — and even a lush lawn looks unkempt without them.

## It's not just looks

A defined edge does real work:

- **Keeps grass out of beds.** Edging stops turf from creeping into mulch and flower beds, where it competes with your plants.
- **Makes mowing cleaner.** A clear edge gives a clean line to mow and trim to, every week.
- **Slows weeds.** A crisp bed edge is a small but real barrier against grass and weeds migrating in.

## Crisp lines, every visit

Edging isn't a once-a-year job. Grass grows back over the line within a couple of weeks, so the edge has to be maintained to stay sharp. That's why we re-edge driveways, walkways, and beds as part of regular maintenance — not as an occasional add-on.

It's the detail our customers mention most: *come home and your house is edged perfectly, looking crispier than all the neighbors.* That's not an accident. It's the edge.
