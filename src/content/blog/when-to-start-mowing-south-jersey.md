The first cut of the year sets the tone for the whole season. Start too early and you stress a lawn that's still waking up; wait too long and you're fighting tall, clumpy growth. In South Jersey, the sweet spot usually lands in **late March to early April**, once the grass is actively growing and the soil has dried out enough to hold a mower without rutting.

## Watch the grass, not the calendar

Forget a fixed date. Your lawn will tell you when it's ready. Once cool-season grasses hit about **3 to 3.5 inches tall** and are growing steadily, it's time for the first cut. If you can mow without the tires sinking in and the clippings aren't matting into wet clumps, you're good to go.

## How short is too short?

This is the mistake we see most: cutting too low. It feels efficient — mow shorter, mow less often — but a scalped lawn is a stressed lawn. Short grass has shallow roots, dries out faster, and leaves bare soil where weeds move in.

Stick to the **one-third rule**: never remove more than a third of the blade in a single cut. For most South Jersey lawns, that means keeping your grass around **3 to 4 inches** through the season. Taller grass shades the soil, holds moisture, and crowds out crabgrass before it starts.

## A few first-cut habits that pay off

- **Sharpen the blade.** A dull blade tears grass instead of cutting it, leaving frayed brown tips.
- **Clear the lawn first.** Sticks and winter debris turn into projectiles and dull your blade.
- **Don't bag every time.** Mulched clippings return nitrogen to the soil.
- **Edge from the start.** A crisp edge makes even an early-season lawn look cared-for.

Get the first few cuts right and the rest of the season is a lot easier — for you and your lawn.
