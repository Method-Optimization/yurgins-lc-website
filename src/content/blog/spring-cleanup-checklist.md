Winter is hard on a yard. Wet leaves mat down the lawn, beds fill with debris, and shrubs come out of dormancy looking ragged. A good spring cleanup clears all of it and sets your property up for a strong season. Here's the order we work in.

## 1. Clear the debris

Start by removing everything winter left behind: matted leaves, fallen branches, and the buildup that smothers grass and invites disease. Raking also lifts the lawn and lets air and light reach the crowns.

## 2. Cut back and shape

Late winter into early spring is the right window to prune most shrubs and remove dead or damaged limbs before new growth takes off. Cleaning up now means the energy goes into healthy growth, not deadwood.

## 3. Re-edge and refresh the beds

Beds are what make a property look *finished*. Re-cut the edges for a crisp line, pull early weeds at the root, and refresh mulch where it's thinned out. A clean edge and fresh mulch do more for curb appeal than almost anything else.

## 4. Give the lawn its first attention

A light first mow, a check for bare or thin spots, and a plan for the season. Catching problems now — compaction, drainage, early weeds — is far easier than fixing them in July.

## Your quick checklist

- [ ] Remove leaves, sticks, and debris
- [ ] Prune shrubs and dead limbs
- [ ] Re-edge all beds
- [ ] Weed at the root
- [ ] Refresh mulch
- [ ] First mow + bare-spot check
- [ ] Haul away all green waste
